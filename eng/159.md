Before tinkering with how the game works, it’s important to play. Play to find out what happens when you push the game in different directions. Play to reveal the hidden connections between moves. Play to develop an appreciation of the environment. That way, when you start mucking about, whether it’s tweaking the basic moves or writing a new Skin, you’ll have an intuitive appreciation of how your changes will ripple through the rest of the design. And when you take your ideas into playtesting, you’ll have a better baseline for detecting wonky side-effects.
